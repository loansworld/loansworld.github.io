Great Lakes Student Loans: How to Stay Organized

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Student loans are a reality for millions of Americans, and managing them effectively can be overwhelming. Great Lakes is one of the largest federal student loan servicers, helping borrowers navigate repayment plans, forgiveness programs, and financial challenges. With rising inflation, economic uncertainty, and evolving student loan policies, staying organized is more critical than ever.

Why Organization Matters for Student Loan Borrowers

The average student loan debt in the U.S. exceeds $37,000 per borrower, and without a solid repayment strategy, it’s easy to fall behind. Missed payments can lead to penalties, higher interest, and even damage to your credit score. By staying organized, you can:

  • Avoid late payments
  • Qualify for forgiveness programs
  • Reduce financial stress
  • Plan for long-term financial goals

The Impact of Student Loan Policies in 2024

Recent changes in federal student loan policies, including the Biden administration’s debt relief initiatives and adjustments to income-driven repayment (IDR) plans, make it essential to stay informed. Borrowers who track their loans closely can take advantage of new opportunities, such as:

  • Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) improvements
  • One-time account adjustments for IDR forgiveness
  • Lower monthly payments under the SAVE Plan

Step-by-Step Guide to Organizing Your Great Lakes Student Loans

1. Understand Your Loan Details

Start by logging into your Great Lakes account to review:

  • Loan types (Federal Direct, Perkins, FFEL, etc.)
  • Interest rates (fixed or variable)
  • Current balance and repayment status

If you have multiple loans, consider consolidating them for simpler management.

2. Set Up Automatic Payments

Enrolling in auto-debit ensures you never miss a payment and may qualify you for a 0.25% interest rate reduction.

3. Choose the Right Repayment Plan

Great Lakes offers several repayment options:

  • Standard Repayment (fixed payments over 10 years)
  • Graduated Repayment (payments increase over time)
  • Income-Driven Repayment (IDR) (payments based on earnings)

Evaluate which plan aligns with your financial situation.

4. Track Deadlines and Important Dates

Use a digital calendar or budgeting app to note:

  • Payment due dates
  • Annual recertification deadlines (for IDR plans)
  • PSLF employment certification deadlines

5. Keep Records of All Communications

Save emails, letters, and call logs from Great Lakes. If disputes arise, documentation is crucial.

6. Explore Forgiveness and Assistance Programs

Check if you qualify for:

  • Teacher Loan Forgiveness
  • Closed School Discharge
  • Total and Permanent Disability (TPD) Discharge

Tools to Help You Stay on Track

Budgeting Apps

Apps like Mint, YNAB (You Need A Budget), or PocketGuard can sync with your loan accounts to track payments.

Spreadsheets

Create a custom spreadsheet to monitor:

  • Principal vs. interest payments
  • Progress toward forgiveness
  • Estimated payoff date

Great Lakes Mobile App

Download the Great Lakes app for on-the-go access to:

  • Account balances
  • Payment history
  • Repayment plan details

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Ignoring Loan Servicer Updates

Policy changes happen frequently. Check your email and Great Lakes account regularly for updates.

Skipping Annual IDR Recertification

Missing the deadline can result in higher payments or removal from the IDR plan.

Not Checking for Errors

Review your loan statements for discrepancies in:

  • Payment amounts
  • Interest accrual
  • Forgiveness credit counts

The Future of Student Loans

With ongoing debates about student debt cancellation, interest rate reforms, and refinancing options, borrowers must stay proactive. Whether through legislative changes or personal financial strategies, organization remains the key to managing student loans successfully.

By implementing these strategies, you can take control of your Great Lakes student loans and work toward a debt-free future.

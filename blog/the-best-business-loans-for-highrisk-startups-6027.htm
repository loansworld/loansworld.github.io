The Best Business Loans for High-Risk Startups

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Starting a business is never easy, but launching a high-risk startup comes with even greater financial challenges. Whether you're in industries like cannabis, fintech, or any sector with unpredictable cash flow, securing funding can feel like an uphill battle. Traditional lenders often shy away from high-risk ventures, leaving entrepreneurs scrambling for alternative solutions.

Fortunately, several financing options cater specifically to high-risk startups. From online lenders to revenue-based financing, this guide explores the best business loans available to help your venture thrive—even when banks say no.

Why High-Risk Startups Struggle with Traditional Financing

High-risk startups face unique hurdles when applying for loans. Understanding these challenges can help you navigate the lending landscape more effectively.

Industries Considered High-Risk

Lenders categorize certain industries as high-risk due to regulatory scrutiny, market volatility, or inconsistent revenue streams. Common high-risk sectors include:
- Cannabis and CBD businesses (due to federal legality issues in the U.S.)
- Fintech and cryptocurrency startups (fraud and regulatory risks)
- Restaurants and hospitality (high failure rates)
- E-commerce and dropshipping (intense competition and thin margins)

Common Reasons for Loan Denials

  • Poor or limited credit history – Many startups lack an established business credit profile.
  • Unpredictable cash flow – Lenders prefer stable revenue streams.
  • Legal and regulatory risks – Industries like cannabis face banking restrictions.

Top Loan Options for High-Risk Startups

If traditional banks reject your application, don’t lose hope. Here are the best financing alternatives for high-risk businesses.

1. Online Business Loans

Online lenders offer faster approvals and more flexible requirements than banks. Some top options include:
- Fundbox – Short-term loans and lines of credit with minimal credit requirements.
- Kabbage (by American Express) – Revenue-based financing for businesses with at least $3,000/month in revenue.
- OnDeck – Term loans and lines of credit for startups with at least one year in operation.

Pros:
✔ Fast funding (sometimes within 24 hours)
✔ Less stringent credit requirements
✔ Flexible repayment terms

Cons:
✘ Higher interest rates than bank loans
✘ Shorter repayment periods

2. Merchant Cash Advances (MCAs)

If your business processes credit card payments, an MCA could be a viable option. Instead of a traditional loan, you receive a lump sum in exchange for a percentage of future sales.

Best for:
- Retail stores
- Restaurants
- Seasonal businesses

Pros:
✔ No collateral required
✔ Approval based on sales, not credit
✔ Quick access to capital

Cons:
✘ Extremely high APRs (sometimes over 100%)
✘ Daily or weekly repayments can strain cash flow

3. Revenue-Based Financing

This model allows startups to repay loans as a percentage of monthly revenue. It’s ideal for businesses with fluctuating income.

Top Providers:
- Clearbanc – Funds e-commerce and SaaS startups.
- Lighter Capital – Specializes in tech and recurring-revenue businesses.

Pros:
✔ Payments adjust with revenue (flexible during slow months)
✔ No personal credit check required

Cons:
✘ Higher cost than traditional loans
✘ Not suitable for businesses with very low revenue

4. Equipment Financing

If your startup needs machinery, vehicles, or tech hardware, equipment financing allows you to borrow against the asset itself.

Best for:
- Construction companies
- Medical practices
- Manufacturing startups

Pros:
✔ Easier approval (the equipment serves as collateral)
✔ Fixed interest rates

Cons:
✘ Limited to equipment purchases
✘ Risk of repossession if payments are missed

5. Crowdfunding & Peer-to-Peer (P2P) Lending

Platforms like Kickstarter, Indiegogo, and Funding Circle allow startups to raise capital from individual investors rather than institutions.

Best for:
- Consumer product startups
- Social impact businesses

Pros:
✔ No credit checks
✔ Potential for viral marketing

Cons:
✘ Requires strong marketing effort
✘ All-or-nothing funding models (on some platforms)

How to Improve Your Chances of Approval

Even high-risk startups can boost their loan eligibility with the right strategies.

Build Strong Business Credit

  • Open a business credit card
  • Pay vendors on time
  • Monitor your credit reports (Experian, Dun & Bradstreet)

Show Consistent Revenue

Lenders want proof that your business can repay the loan. If possible, wait until you have at least six months of revenue before applying.

Consider a Co-Signer or Collateral

If your personal credit is strong, adding a co-signer can increase approval odds. Alternatively, offering collateral (like real estate or inventory) reduces the lender’s risk.

Explore SBA Loans (If Eligible)

While traditional SBA loans are tough for high-risk businesses, some lenders offer SBA Community Advantage Loans for underserved markets.

Final Thoughts

Securing funding for a high-risk startup isn’t impossible—it just requires creativity and persistence. By exploring alternative lenders, optimizing your financials, and choosing the right loan type, you can secure the capital needed to grow your business.

Remember: The best loan depends on your industry, revenue, and long-term goals. Research thoroughly, compare offers, and don’t settle for predatory terms. With the right strategy, even high-risk ventures can thrive.

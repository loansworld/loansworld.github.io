Cash App Loan Requirements: Do You Qualify?

Home / Blog / Blog Details

In today’s fast-paced digital economy, financial flexibility is more important than ever. Whether you’re dealing with an unexpected expense, trying to bridge a gap between paychecks, or looking to invest in a side hustle, having access to quick cash can be a game-changer. Cash App, the popular mobile payment service, has stepped into the lending space with its Cash App Borrow feature, offering eligible users short-term loans. But who qualifies for these loans, and what are the requirements?

What Is Cash App Borrow?

Cash App Borrow is a feature within the Cash App ecosystem that allows eligible users to take out small, short-term loans—typically ranging from $20 to $200. Unlike traditional loans, Cash App Borrow doesn’t require a credit check, making it an attractive option for those with less-than-perfect credit. However, the feature isn’t available to all users, and eligibility depends on several factors.

How Does Cash App Borrow Work?

If you qualify, you can request a loan directly within the app. Here’s a quick breakdown of how it works:

  1. Check Eligibility – Open Cash App, navigate to the Banking tab, and see if the "Borrow" option appears.
  2. Select Loan Amount – If eligible, you can choose how much you want to borrow (within the offered limit).
  3. Repayment Terms – Loans must typically be repaid within four weeks, with a 5% flat fee (or higher, depending on extensions).
  4. Automatic Repayment – Cash App will automatically deduct the owed amount (plus fees) from your linked debit card or balance on the due date.

Who Qualifies for a Cash App Loan?

Not everyone has access to Cash App Borrow. The company uses an algorithm-based system to determine eligibility, considering factors like:

1. Account Activity & History

Cash App prefers users who:
- Have an active account for several months.
- Receive regular deposits (e.g., direct deposits from employers or frequent peer-to-peer transactions).
- Maintain a positive balance history.

2. Geographic Availability

As of now, Cash App Borrow is not available in all U.S. states. Some users in restricted regions won’t see the option, regardless of their account standing.

3. Creditworthiness (Without a Credit Check)

While Cash App doesn’t perform a hard credit pull, it may assess your financial behavior within the app, such as:
- How often you overdraft.
- Whether you’ve repaid previous Cash App loans on time.
- Your overall transaction volume.

4. Direct Deposit Enrollment

Users who set up direct deposit with Cash App are more likely to qualify. This signals steady income, reducing the risk for lenders.

Why Might You Be Denied?

Even if you meet some of the criteria above, you might still be ineligible. Common reasons include:

Insufficient Account Activity

New or inactive accounts rarely qualify. Cash App wants to see consistent usage before extending credit.

Poor Repayment History

If you’ve previously borrowed from Cash App and missed payments, you may be blocked from future loans.

State Restrictions

Certain states have strict lending laws, making Cash App Borrow unavailable. Always check local regulations.

Alternatives If You Don’t Qualify

If Cash App Borrow isn’t an option for you, consider these alternatives:

1. Payday Alternative Loans (PALs)

Offered by some credit unions, these small-dollar loans have lower interest rates than traditional payday loans.

2. Earnin or Dave Apps

These apps provide cash advances based on your upcoming paycheck, often with no interest (but optional tips).

3. Personal Loans from Online Lenders

Companies like Upstart or LendingClub offer personal loans with more flexible terms, though they do require credit checks.

The Risks of Cash App Borrow

While convenient, Cash App loans come with risks:

High Fees for Late Payments

If you don’t repay on time, fees can stack up quickly, making the loan much more expensive.

Limited Borrowing Amounts

With a max of $200, these loans won’t cover major emergencies.

Potential for Debt Cycles

Short-term loans can trap borrowers in a cycle of borrowing and repaying, especially if income is unstable.

Final Thoughts: Is Cash App Borrow Right for You?

If you need a small, quick loan and meet the eligibility criteria, Cash App Borrow can be a useful tool. However, always weigh the costs and explore alternatives before committing. Financial responsibility is key—borrow only what you can repay.
